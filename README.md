# useful-for-me

Small bodies of work that helped me, shared in case they help others.

---

## 1. Awareness — Why This Exists

Some things become useful enough that I want to keep them somewhere public.

This repository is for those things: notes, essays, experiments, small projects, and practical artifacts that helped me think more clearly, act more directly, or compress a piece of work into a form I can return to.

## 2. Limitations — What This Is Not

This is not a claim of authority. It is not a polished publication venue. It is not medical, psychological, legal, financial, or professional advice. If a problem is serious, clinical, urgent, or high-stakes, talk to the relevant professional.

I proofread the writing, but I do not polish it to formal-publication standards. This repository is called "useful for me" for a reason: these are working artifacts, not finished papers on Zenodo or arXiv.

## 3. Focus — The Standard

The standard here is usefulness.

Useful does not mean final. Useful does not mean universally true. Useful means that the work clarified something, changed something, or gave me an operation I could actually use.

## 4. What Is Here

This is AI-augmented content.

Most of the writing here comes out of long discussions with AI. I am not a fast writer, so I use AI as a writing partner, but the work is not casual generation.

The ideas are explored through conversation: questioned, tested, refined, rejected where they do not hold, and kept when the shape of the idea feels aligned. The resulting pieces are a way to compress the knowledge produced by that work.

## 5. Alignment with Existing Knowledge

Some pieces use academic structure because that format is useful for compressing and testing ideas. Citations and scientific claims should be treated as good-faith working notes unless independently verified.

## 6. Agency — How To Use This Repository

Each folder is intended to stand on its own. Start with the folder README, then read or use the material in whatever order that README recommends.

Current example:

- [Procrastination](procrastination/) — a seven-paper investigation into procrastination: what it is, why the usual deficit frames fail, and what operations make stalled tasks lighter.

## 7. Flexibility — What May Appear Here

The contents may change shape over time. Some folders may be polished writing, some may be working notes, some may be practical tools, and some may be experiments that only make sense in their own context.

The organizing principle is not format. The organizing principle is whether the thing was useful enough to preserve.

## 8. Conclusion — The Whole

Here is what I have. Might be useful.
