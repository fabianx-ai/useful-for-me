# Procrastination

Working notes from a personal investigation into procrastination — what it actually is, why the standard frames fail, and what operations turn out to make it lighter.

These started as conversations with Claude over a few days. The investigation was driven by a specific question: *why do capable, motivated, disciplined people fail to do small, simple tasks they genuinely want done?* The standard answers (lack of willpower, focus, discipline) don't survive contact with the evidence — the same person who can't pay an invoice can memorize two hundred football names, beat a hard video game, run a business. Something else is going on.

The papers that follow work out what that something else is, layer by layer. Each paper addresses one mechanism. Together they describe a complete operation.

## What's here

- **01 — Awareness:** *what procrastination is and is not.* Establishes the diagnosis. Procrastination is not a deficit; it is a withdrawal of presence from tasks whose execution exposes the self to internal judgment.

- **02 — Freedom:** *the judge is a measurement device that got asked the wrong question.* Identifies the internal judge as a comparator conscripted into a job it was never designed for, and gives the operation for returning it to its proper function.

- **03 — Focus:** *the attention layer and the asymmetric record.* Names the second mechanism — attention's tendency to register errors heavily and process successes as baseline — and gives the rebalancing operation.

- **04 — Presence:** *the clean transformation of wave to particle.* Addresses the fear of making work real. The particle contains the wave; more draws produce a more complete picture of the wave; present early and often.

- **05 — Alignment:** *the joy of moving in your direction.* Reframes alignment from state to direction. Joy is the indicator. Move where joy points, share what you are doing, trust happiness as the steady state.

- **06 — Agency:** *why to-do lists fail and queues restore agency.* The data structure was the problem, not the user. Lists demand O(n) cognitive operations at every viewing; priority queues with partial view are O(1). Replace the structure, restore the agency.

- **07 — Flexibility:** *imagination in service of truth.* The wave must stay open for the optimal path to emerge. Premature collapse — through forced evaluation or imagination-as-completion — forecloses on the path that would have emerged through wave dynamics. Imagination as compass, action as drawing, truth as what emerges.

## How to read these

I have decided to organize my thinking as academic papers. Academic papers have a certain structure that is useful for me to compress knowledge. If psychologists find them useful they could become real papers, but they don't need to.

Read them in order if you can — each paper builds on the previous ones — but each one also stands on its own. Or let your AI agent summarize them for you.

The science citations in each paper's section 5 are characterized accurately to the best of my knowledge but have not been formally verified. If you find an error, I'd appreciate knowing.

The papers were tested in real life, on actual undone tasks, while being written. They worked for me. They might work for you. They might not. That is the appropriate level of certainty to apply.

## What this is not

Not a self-help system. Not a productivity method. Not a treatment for clinical conditions. If you are dealing with significant mental health challenges, the right thing to do is talk to a professional, not read essays from a stranger's GitHub.

## The posture

Mendel published his pea experiments in a regional journal with essentially the attitude of *this might be interesting*. He was ignored for thirty-five years. Then someone rediscovered his work and founded a field.

This is in that posture. Here is what I have. Might be useful.
